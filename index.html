ABOUT THE FISHER CENTER ANTI-RACISM AND ACCESSIBILITY WORKING GROUPS

The Anti-Racism and Accessibility Working Groups are composed of volunteers from across The Fisher Center who are dedicated to addressing and dismantling systemic barriers, fostering a sense of belonging, and advocating for diversity in the performing arts. We have been involved in initiatives such as replacing our Assistive Listening Device system in our Sosnoff and LUMA theaters; creating, publishing, and maintaining a “Know Before You Go” guide, which gives a comprehensive overview of every aspect of attending a performance; and continue to strengthen partnerships between the Fisher Center and Bard College-based organizations like Rethinking Place: Bard-on-Mahicantuck, The Center for Indigenous Studies, The Council for Inclusive Excellence, and BardEATS. We work towards holding ourselves and our community accountable to combat racism and improve accessibility through our practices and programming at the Fisher Center.
